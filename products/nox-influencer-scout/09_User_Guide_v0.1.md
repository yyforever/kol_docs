## Conclusion

If this is your first time opening this guide, do not try to learn every feature first.

Just remember this:

**NoxInfluencer Scout is not only a data panel. It is a browser workflow that helps you go from “check a creator -> find similar creators -> save good candidates -> keep moving the follow-up forward” while you browse YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram.**

For your first try, do these 3 things:

1. Open a creator profile you already know and look at the on-page insights
2. Click `Find Similar` once and see what kind of shortlist it gives you
3. Save one good creator to favorites or outreach

If those 3 steps make sense, you will understand the product much faster than by clicking every button.

## 1. If You Only Read One Section / 如果你只看一段

NoxInfluencer Scout is best for moments like this:

- You are browsing social platforms and find a creator who looks promising
- You do not want to jump back and forth between tabs, spreadsheets, and search pages
- You want to know quickly whether this creator is worth more time
- If the answer is yes, you want to expand the list and save good candidates right away

The easiest way to think about the extension is:

- It helps you make decisions **while you browse**
- It helps you turn **one creator into a shortlist**
- It helps you **save good candidates before you lose them**
- It hands off heavier execution and management steps **to the main website**

So on your first day, do not focus on “how many features are in the product.”
Focus on:

- Can I judge a creator faster?
- Can I expand one good lead into more options?
- Can I save the good ones before I forget them?

## 2. What This Extension Is For / 这是什么，适合谁

### What it is

NoxInfluencer Scout is a browser extension that works directly while you browse `YouTube`, `TikTok`, and `Instagram`.

Its 4 core jobs are:

- show creator data directly on the page
- help you find similar creators from the current profile
- let you save good creators to favorites or outreach
- show lightweight CRM context and hand off heavier steps to the website

### Who it is for

This extension is a better fit if you are:

- browsing social platforms and screening creators in real time
- starting from one seed creator and trying to expand the list quickly
- trying to avoid splitting “view, compare, save, and follow up” across too many tools

### What it is not

To avoid the wrong expectation:

- it is not a full standalone back-office system
- it is not just a static data card with no next steps
- it is not a pricing page or a sales brochure

## 3. Your First 3 Minutes / 第一次打开，先这样用

### Step 1: Install and pin it

- Install the extension in Chrome
- Pin it to the browser toolbar

This matters more than it sounds. Many users do install the extension, but later fail to use it simply because they cannot find it quickly.

### Step 2: Sign in first

- Open the extension
- Sign in with your NoxInfluencer account

The extension and the website use the same account system.

### Step 3: Do not click everything yet

The best first run is not “open every tab.”

Start with one creator profile you already know, then do this:

1. check the on-page data panel
2. click `Find Similar`
3. save one good creator

That is the fastest way to feel whether the product is useful.

## 4. Where It Shows Up / 它会在哪些页面出现

### Where you will usually see it

The extension mainly appears on:

- creator profile or channel pages
- creator detail-related pages
- the extension sidebar
- the extension popup panel

### What you will usually see

- on a creator page, you will first see insights and action entry points
- after clicking `Find Similar`, you will enter the search and results flow in the sidebar
- some actions will continue on the website when they become heavier execution steps

### If you do not see the extension

Do not assume it is broken first. Check these things:

- is it installed and signed in
- is it pinned to the Chrome toolbar
- are you on a supported platform and page

## 5. Check the Data First / 先看数据，再决定要不要继续

### Why start with the data

When you land on a creator page, your real question is usually simple:

**Is this creator worth more of my time?**

The on-page data panel is there to answer that first.

### What you will usually see

You will normally see:

- basic performance data
- follower count, tags, and basic profile info
- average views, average engagement rate, and posting frequency
- entry points to audience and business-related insights
- actions like favorite, outreach, and similar search

### How to read the current data

You do not need to understand everything on day one.

For your first use, just know this:

- average views and engagement are shown by default based on the **latest 10 videos**
- if switching is available, you can move to other time windows
- the metric logic should stay aligned with the website

Your first goal is not “understand every metric.”
Your first goal is simply: when you open a profile, can you quickly tell whether it is worth digging into?

### If the data is incomplete

Sometimes you may see:

- only basic data, without deeper analysis
- data that is still being filled in
- a creator whose profile is not fully collected yet

That does not always mean something is broken. It may simply mean the creator data is incomplete or the deeper layer depends on your account access.

## 6. Use Similar Search to Build a Better Shortlist / 用“找相似”把候选越找越准

### When to click `Find Similar`

The best moment to use `Find Similar` is not when you have no direction at all.

It works best when:

- you already found one creator who feels directionally right
- you feel “this is not my final answer, but this is a good seed”
- you want the system to expand outward from that seed

### The right mindset

`Find Similar` is not a magic button that gives a perfect final answer in one shot.

A better way to think about it is:

- it gives you the first batch of candidates
- you narrow the list
- you use the better results as new seeds
- your shortlist becomes more focused over time

### A good first-time flow

1. open a seed creator profile you agree with
2. click `Find Similar`
3. review the first batch without expecting every result to be perfect
4. narrow the range with filters
5. hide the results you do not want to see again
6. run another similar search from a better match

### What you will see

You will normally see:

- the current seed creator
- a batch of similar results
- filters like platform, follower size, average views, and region
- hidden-result controls
- an entry point to run another round of similar search

### When it starts feeling useful

Not when the first batch is perfect.

It starts feeling useful when:

- it saves you from manual searching again
- the quality improves after you filter once
- it helps you move from “one direction” to “a usable shortlist”

## 7. Save Good Creators Before You Lose Them / 看到合适的，先存下来

### Why this matters

Many people do not lose good creators because they never find them.
They lose them because:

- they saw them
- thought “this one looks good”
- kept scrolling
- forgot them later

So the extension should not only help you view creators. It should help you keep the good ones.

### Favorites vs outreach

The easiest way to think about the difference is:

- Favorites: worth saving, but not ready to act yet
- Outreach: already closer to your priority list and more likely to move forward

If you are still unsure, save to favorites first.
If you already know this creator should move forward, add to outreach.

### What feedback you should see

After saving a creator, the page should clearly show that the action was recorded, for example:

- button state changes
- card state changes
- avatar or list markers

This feedback is not cosmetic. It helps you trust that the candidate is already stored.

### Why batch actions matter

Once you already have a list, clicking one by one becomes slow.
That is why the result flow is gradually adding:

- batch add to favorites
- batch add to outreach

The real value is simple: you no longer have to rely on memory or manual spreadsheets to keep the list alive.

## 8. Why Some Actions Continue on the Website / 为什么有时会跳到主站

### Do not treat the website handoff as a break

On the first day, many users ask:

“Why does this continue on the website instead of staying inside the extension?”

The cleaner way to think about it is:

- the extension handles browsing, judging, filtering, and triggering actions
- the website handles heavier execution, upgrade, and management steps

So the workflow is not broken. It is simply moving from the browsing scene to the execution scene.

### What CRM does inside the extension

The extension-side CRM is more like a lightweight reminder layer. It helps you:

- quickly know whether this creator was already touched before
- view basic status, partial records, or preview content
- avoid repeated judgment and repeated action

### Which actions usually continue on the website

Common directions include:

- upgrade-related actions: upgrade or pricing pages
- execution-related actions: email tasks or outreach sending pages
- management-related actions: CRM or campaign management pages

### What matters most for the user

The easiest way to remember it is:

- use the extension to **discover**
- use the website to **execute**

Once you see it this way, the website handoff feels less like a gap and more like the second half of the same workflow.

## 9. Why Your View May Look Different / 为什么你看到的功能和别人不一样

### The most common reason

If you notice that:

- someone else sees a feature that you do not
- a section is visible but locked
- another user can see deeper data while you only see the basic layer

the most common reason is not a bug.
It is usually the difference in account state and feature access.

### The common cases

#### Not signed in

You can normally see part of the basic video and creator information.

#### Free user

After signing in, you can see more creator data and get limited access to selected product flows.

#### Paid user

You can use the more complete audience insights, business-related data, similar search, and CRM features.

### Why you may see “unlock” or upgrade prompts

That usually means:

- the feature belongs to a higher access level
- the feature has limited trial access
- the action continues on the website

You do not need to study pricing details here.
You mainly need to know:

- what you can do now
- which step needs more access
- which step continues on the website

## 10. FAQ / 常见问题

### Why is the extension not showing up?

Check these first:

- is it installed and signed in
- is it pinned to the Chrome toolbar
- are you on a supported page

### Why is some creator data incomplete?

Common reasons include:

- the creator data is still being filled in
- the platform does not expose every layer equally
- the deeper layer depends on your account access

### Why are the similar results not exactly what I expected?

Because similar search is a candidate expansion tool, not an exact-match tool.
The better flow is:

- accept the first batch
- narrow it with filters
- hide the results you do not want
- start another round from a better match

### Why does it sometimes jump to the website?

Because the extension handles browsing and judgment first, while the website handles heavier execution and management.
