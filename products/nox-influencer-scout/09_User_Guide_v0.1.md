> Updated: 2026-04-08

If this is your first time reading this guide, don't try to learn every feature at once.
Just remember one thing: **NoxInfluencer Scout is not a "look at data and leave" extension. It helps you go from "check data → find similar creators → save good candidates → manage follow-ups" in one seamless flow while you browse YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram.**
For your first try, do these 3 things:
1. Open a creator profile you already know and check the on-page data panel
2. Click `Find Similar` once and see what kind of shortlist it gives you
3. Save one good creator to favorites or outreach

Once those 3 steps click, you'll know pretty quickly whether the extension is worth keeping.

## 2. What This Extension Is For

### What it is
NoxInfluencer Scout is a Chrome browser extension that works directly while you browse YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram.
It does 4 things:
- Shows creator data right on the page — avg views, engagement rate, audience overview — so you don't have to calculate anything yourself
- Lets you find similar creators from the current profile in one click
- Lets you save good creators to favorites or outreach instead of copying them into a spreadsheet
- Shows lightweight CRM status right inside the extension, with heavier actions continuing on the main website

### Who it works best for
- **Outreach specialists and BD reps** who spend hours every day browsing platforms to find creators
- **Brand owners or sellers** who have one or two seed creators and want to quickly expand to dozens or hundreds of candidates
- **Small teams** tired of switching between "browse on the platform, record in Excel, look up data in the backend"

### What it is not
Setting expectations up front:
- It is not a full back-office system — heavier workflows continue on the main website
- It is not a static data card with no next steps — after viewing data, you can take action

## 4. 🔥 Check the Data — Judge a Creator in Seconds [Must Read]

### Why start with the data
When you land on a creator's page, you really only have one question: **Is this person worth more of my time?**
Before, you probably had to scroll through five or six videos, mentally estimate the average views, and then switch to another tool to check the audience. The data panel does that in one shot.

### What you'll see
When you open a creator profile, the data panel appears on the page automatically:
- **Avg Views**: calculated from the latest 10 videos by default (you can switch the window). This is the most direct indicator of a creator's real everyday traffic — don't be fooled by accounts with millions of followers but only a few thousand views per video
- **Engagement Rate (ER)**: the ratio of interactions to views, reflecting how active the audience actually is. Higher ER means the followers aren't just passing by — they're genuinely engaging
- **Posting Frequency**: how often they publish. If you're planning a collaboration next month but they haven't posted in three months, that's a red flag
- **Follower count, tags**: basic profile info to help you quickly identify what kind of creator this is
- **Audience analysis entry**: deeper data on gender, age, and geographic distribution
- **Business data entry**: estimated pricing and other commercial reference points

### 🎯 A Word on Audience Analysis — This One Matters
This deserves its own callout because a lot of people get tripped up here.
In the age of recommendation algorithms, a creator's "followers" and the people who actually see and engage with their content are no longer the same group. A US-based beauty creator might have 1 million followers, but the algorithm pushes her videos to a large audience in Southeast Asia. If you only look at the follower demographics from her platform dashboard, you'd think the audience is in the US. But the people actually watching, liking, and commenting might be somewhere else entirely.
Our audience analysis is based on **the profiles of users who actually engage** — these aren't people who scrolled past after 2 seconds. They watched the content, liked it, left comments, saved it. In other words, they are the audience that actually has a chance of seeing your marketing message.
So **if you notice that our audience data doesn't match what the creator sees in their own platform dashboard, don't jump to the conclusion that our data is wrong.** The two use different methodologies: the platform dashboard leans toward follower composition, while ours tracks the actual engaged audience. For your campaign decisions, the latter is the group you're really trying to reach.
The worst thing in influencer marketing is spending a US-market budget and ending up with traffic from regions that aren't your target. Take the audience analysis step seriously.

### 💡 Tips
- **You don't need to understand everything on day one.** Start with two numbers: avg views and ER. Avg views tells you the traffic scale, ER tells you the traffic quality — together, that's enough for a first-pass judgment
- There's a **refresh button** at the bottom of the panel. If the data looks stale, tap it to pull the latest numbers

### If the data is incomplete
Sometimes you'll see only basic data without audience analysis, or a creator whose profile is still being filled in. That's not necessarily a problem — it may mean the creator was recently indexed and data is still being processed, or the deeper analysis layer depends on your current account access.

## 5. 🔥 Find Similar — Build a Better Shortlist Round by Round [Must Read]

### When to click `Find Similar`
The best time is not when you have no direction at all. It works best when:
- You've found a creator who feels directionally right
- You think "this isn't my final answer, but it's a good seed"
- You want the system to expand outward from that direction

If you don't even have a direction yet, keep browsing a few more channels. Start once you've found the first one that feels right.

### What you'll see
After clicking `Find Similar`, a sidebar search area opens. You can set filters:
- **Region**: where your target audience is
- **Follower count**: drag the slider to set a range
- **Avg views**: set a minimum and maximum
- **Platforms**: YouTube / TikTok / Instagram (multi-select)

Once you've set the filters, click `Find Similar from [seed creator]`. Results appear as cards, one at a time. You can mark each one `Interested` or `Not Interested`, or just click `Next` to keep going.

### 🔑 Pro Tip
**If you spot a creator during filtering who's an even better fit than your original seed, don't wait — click their `Find Similar` button right away and use them as the new seed.** This is "chain discovery": the more accurate your seed, the more accurate the next batch. Your shortlist gets sharper with every round.

### The right mindset
`Find Similar` is not a magic button that delivers a perfect list in one shot. A better way to think about it:
1. The first batch gives you a general direction
2. You narrow the range with filters
3. You hide the results you don't want to see again
4. You start another round from a better match
5. After a few rounds, the quality improves noticeably

Anyone who's been running influencer campaigns for a few months knows: a good shortlist is never found in one search. It's built round by round. This tool just turns that manual process into a semi-automated one.

### When it starts feeling useful
Not when the first batch is perfect. It starts feeling useful when:
- You no longer have to search manually
- The quality is clearly better after one round of filtering
- You've gone from "one direction" to "a workable shortlist"

## 6. Save Good Creators Before You Lose Them

### Why this matters
Every outreach specialist has been through this:
- Spotted a great creator
- Thought "this one looks good"
- Kept scrolling
- Completely forgot who it was 10 minutes later

The extension doesn't just help you view creators. It helps you keep the good ones.

### Favorites vs outreach
Simple rule:
- **Favorites**: worth saving — you want to come back later
- **Outreach**: already on your priority list — you'll probably move forward with them

Not sure yet? Save to favorites.
Pretty sure they're a fit? Add to outreach.

### What feedback you'll see
After saving a creator, the page gives clear feedback — button state changes, card markers. That's not decoration. It's confirmation that the action was recorded.

### Batch actions
Once you have a batch of results, clicking one by one gets slow. The results list supports batch add to favorites and batch add to outreach.
No more relying on memory or manual spreadsheets to keep the candidate list alive.
If you need to export emails, create email tasks, or handle other heavier execution steps, those continue on the main website — the extension handles discovery and filtering, the website handles execution and management. Two halves of the same relay.

## 7. CRM: Know the Follow-Up Status the Moment You Open a Profile

### What problem it solves
After a few months of running influencer outreach, you know the biggest headache isn't finding people. It's things like:
- When did I last contact this creator? How far did the conversation get?
- Is someone else on my team already talking to this person?
- What was the last quoted price? Which spreadsheet was that note in?

That information used to be scattered across Excel files, chat threads, and your own memory. Now, when you open a creator's profile and switch to the **CRM tab** in the data panel, it's all right there.

### What you can see and do
- **Collaboration status**: New / Contacted / Negotiating / Signed / Rejected — instantly know what stage this person is at
- **Tags**: add custom tags to creators for easy categorization and retrieval
- **Notes**: jot down quick reminders like "quoted $500", "only does product exchange", "slow to reply"
- **Team activity**: see whether other team members have already been in touch with this creator — avoid two people reaching out to the same influencer

All changes save instantly and sync with the main website. Update a status in the extension and it updates on the website too — and vice versa.
For more complete CRM management — batch editing, full collaboration history, detailed email records — continue on the main website. The CRM inside the extension is a "quick reminder layer": the moment you open a profile, you know the follow-up status without switching to the backend.

## 8. Why Your View May Look Different

### The most common reason
If you notice that:
- Someone else sees a feature that you don't
- A section is visible but locked when you tap it
- Another user sees deeper data while you only see basics

The most common reason is not a bug. It's a difference in account status.

### The common cases
**Not signed in**: you can see some basic video data and creator info.
**Free user**: after signing in, you see more creator data and get limited access to core features. For example, Find Similar has 3 free uses per day, and audience analysis plus business data have a one-time trial of 10 uses.
**Paid user**: full access to audience analysis, business data, Find Similar, CRM, and all other features.

### What "unlock" prompts mean
It means the feature requires a higher access level, or your free quota has been used up. You don't need to study pricing details here. Just know:
- What you can do right now
- Which step requires more access
- Which step continues on the main website

## 9. FAQ

### Why is the extension not showing up?
Check these first:
- Is it installed and signed in?
- Is it pinned to the Chrome toolbar?
- Are you on a creator profile page on YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram?

### Why is some creator data incomplete?
Possible reasons:
- The creator was recently indexed and data is still being processed
- The platform limits what data can be accessed
- Deeper data depends on your account access level

### Why are the similar results not exactly what I expected?
`Find Similar` is a candidate expansion tool, not an exact-match tool. The recommended flow:
1. Accept the first batch
2. Narrow with filters
3. Hide results you don't want
4. Start a new round from a better match

After a few rounds, the quality improves noticeably.

### Are CRM changes synced with the main website?
Yes. Status, tags, and notes you edit in the extension sync instantly to the main website, and vice versa.

### What if Find Similar returns no results?
Try:
- Loosening the filters — widen the follower range, remove the region constraint
- Using a different seed creator
- Confirming the seed creator is on a supported platform

### Why do some actions jump to the main website?
The extension handles browsing, judging, and filtering — the "light" actions. Exporting emails, sending batch emails, and full CRM management are "heavier" actions that continue on the main website. The two sides are a relay, not a disconnect.
